Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about physiotherapy and our services.

The number of sessions varies depending on your condition, its severity, and your individual response to treatment. After your initial assessment, your physiotherapist will provide a recommended treatment plan with an estimated number of sessions. Most acute conditions require 4-6 sessions, while chronic conditions may need longer-term management.
In most cases, you don't need a doctor's referral to see a physiotherapist. However, some insurance plans may require a referral for reimbursement. We recommend checking with your insurance provider. If we determine your condition requires medical attention beyond physiotherapy, we'll refer you to the appropriate healthcare professional.
Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated. For lower body issues, shorts are ideal. For upper body treatments, a tank top or loose t-shirt works well. We have private changing rooms available if needed. Athletic shoes are recommended if we'll be doing exercises.
Many extended health insurance plans cover physiotherapy services. Coverage amounts vary by plan, so we recommend checking with your provider. We provide detailed receipts that you can submit for reimbursement. We also accept direct billing from some insurance companies - ask our front desk for details.
We strive to see new patients within 24-48 hours for urgent cases. Our online booking system shows real-time availability, and we maintain a cancellation list for patients who want to be notified of earlier appointments. Emergency cases are accommodated same-day whenever possible.
